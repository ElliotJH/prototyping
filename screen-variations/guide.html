Part 1: Overview

VAT is a tax you pay on most goods and services.

How much you pay

You pay 20% VAT most of the time - but less on essential items.

Rate % of VAT What the rate applies to
Standard 20% Most goods and services
Reduced rate 5% Some goods and services, eg children’s car seats and home energy
Zero rate 0% Zero-rated goods and services, eg most food and children’s clothes

Some things - eg postage stamps, financial and property transactions - are exempt from VAT.

How VAT is worked out

When someone charges you VAT they multiply their selling price by the VAT rate to calculate the amount of VAT to charge.

They then add this to the selling price to give you the price you actually pay - this is called the ‘gross’ price.

VAT refunds

Self-build new homes or conversions

If you’re building a new home or converting a non-residential building into a home, there’s a VAT refund scheme to help you claim back some of the VAT.

Visitors to the UK

Visitors to the UK can sometimes get VAT refunds through the VAT Retail Export Scheme.

Importing and exporting vehicles

You normally pay VAT when you import a vehicle from inside the European Union (EU), but if you meet certain conditions you don’t have to pay VAT if you import from outside the EU.

Special rules apply if you’re exporting a vehicle outside the EU.
