Making a lasting power of attorney (LPA) is a three step process.

  1. 1. Create an LPA

    Create an LPA

  2. 2. Sign an LPA

  3. 3. Register an LPA

    Register an LPA

What happens next?

There is a minimum six week waiting period before your LPA can be registered and returned to you. It will then be ready for use.

Why the wait?

The minimum six week waiting period is to give people an opportunity to raise any concerns about the LPA (for example, if they think you're being forced into making it, or that a fraud has been committed). You will be asked when you create your LPA who you would like to tell about it.

It also gives the Office of the Public Guardian time to check that the LPA has been made correctly and that there is nothing that would make it invalid, or that should be corrected before it can be registered.

How much does it cost?

The fee to process and register an LPA is £130 for each LPA. People on very low incomes and people who receive certain benefits may be able to pay a lower fee (the online tool will take you through steps to work out if you qualify).
