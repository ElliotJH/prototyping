FORMS LAYOUT

 

Labels

Don't use colons

If your labels are long or vary greatly in length, consider top-alignment

 

Your label should not span more lines than its control

 

Text fields

Avoid shading them.

There's a risk people won't recognise them as fields.

Especially as our buttons are very flat in style.

Also, if the form is on a coloured background they should be left white anyway.

 

Help text

Position help text immediately below the label.

Exeption: For left or right-aligned labels, when the field takes up a single line (for example, a text field or select box) the help text should be positioned immediately below the field.

Exeption: If your field is very short (like a security code) you can position help text to the right of the field)

 

Design

Make label, field and help text the same font size

Label text in bold, help text italic, slightly lighter

Add a little extra padding to text fields

 

Affordance

The general convention is:

• Buttons are raised off the page

• Text fields are cut into the page

 

Fieldsets and legends

• Use colour and size to differentiate legends

• If your labels are right-aligned align your legends so they're directly above the fields

 

===

 

Help is best positioned directly after a label.

It's more likely to be read, especially by screen-reader users.

You can create an 'action - reason' relationship between the label and the help.

 

Help text positioned after their fields have the tone of a reminder.

Something that people should know.

 

Help text positioned after their labels are much more directive - for important instructions rather than a helpful reminder.
