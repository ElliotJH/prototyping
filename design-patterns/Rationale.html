Rationale

 

A range of sites were reviewed to see how others had tackled the problem. Many sites do not ask for this information, which is revealing in itself. The following ten did though: Google+, Facebook, Linkedin, Ebay, Goodreads, Last.fm, EDF Energy, Betfair, National Lottery, Nike+.

Observations

 

• Nine out of ten sites used drop-down lists

• Five sites ran the fields; Day, Month, Year

• Three US sites (Linkedin, Nike+ and Google+) ran the fields Month, Day, Year

• One site (Goodreads) ran the fields Year, Month, Day

• Two sites used free-text fields (EDF and Google+)

• Two sites started the Year drop down at the most recent year that a valid user could be born in

• All Day and Month drop-downs ran forwards, all Year drop-downs ran backwards

• All Year drop-downs went back about 110 years

• Eight sites used left-aligned labels (for all their fields, not just date of birth)

• Two sites didn't visually label the individual fields in any way

• Most sites used the default drop-down value as a label

• Six sites used 'Date of birth' as the label, but four (all American social sites) used 'Birthday'

 

Conclusion

 

We opted to follow the broad convention for UK websites and use three drop downs, ordered Day, Month, Year.
