Apply

There are three online application forms available for this licence:

  1. Application for a premises licence
  2. Provisional statement
  3. Application for need for a Designated Premises Supervisor to be disapplied

1. Application for a premises licence

To supply/sell alcohol, provide regulated entertainment or to provide late-night refreshments in England and Wales you need a licence from the Licensing Authority. These licences are issued for the premises under the provisions of the Licensing Act 2003.

The fee you will pay depends on the information you supply in your application. Guidance on how to calculate the fee is given in the form and you will pay it online. The fee is the same whether you apply online or by post. Businesslink.gov.uk does not charge you for using this service.

What you need to know before you apply

  • There is a £500 licence fee
  • You'll need a debit or credit card

  • You'll need to supply the following documentation:

    • Premises plan (the plans must be to scale and be clearly display the required information)
    • Consent form of premises supervisor (only required if the application includes the sale of alcohol)

Apply online

2. Provisional statement

To provide late-night refreshments and regulated entertainment, and sell alcohol, you need a licence from the local authority if you are in England and Wales. if the premises have not been constructed or are due to go under major refurbishment then an applicaiton for a provisional licence is required.

The fee you will pay depends on the information you supply in your application. Guidance on how to calculate the fee is given in the form and you will pay it online. The fee is the same whether you apply online or by post. Businesslink.gov.uk does not charge you for using this service.

What you need to know before you apply

  • Premises plan (mandatory). The plans must be to scale and be clearly display the required information

Apply on Westminster council's website

3. Application for need for a Designated Premises Supervisor to be disapplied

A Desgnated Premises Supervisor (DPS) is required for a licensed premises to sell alcohol. The DPS is a named induvidual that is responsible for the licence. If the DPS wishes to be removed they must complete this form. No fee is required but they must send a copy of the request to be removed to the licence holder within 48 hours of submission to the licensing authority. Failure to do this will result in the request being denied.

If the DPS requesting to be removed is the licence holder then they must return the premises licence to the authority by attaching a copy to their request. If they are unable to attach a copy of the licence to the application a statement as to why it cannot be supplied must be provided. If the DPS is not the licence holder they must also advise the licence holder that they must send a copy of the licence to the authority within 14 days.

What you need to know before you apply

  • Premises licence (optional). If you cannot provide the licence a statement as to why the licence has not been attached must be included.
